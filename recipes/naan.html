How to Make Naan

Naan is a beautiful Indian flatbread.

Here’s what you need to make the puffiest, fluffiest, bubbliest naan of your life.

  • flour
  • salt
  • milk
  • sugar
  • yeast
  • ghee or butter

Steps

  1. Let’s get the yeast activated and ready to work its magic on our naan dough.
  2. Mix instant yeast with warm water and sugar.
  3. Leave until foamy – Leave the mixture for 10 minutes until it becomes foamy, which means the yeast is alive and kicking.
  4. Mix dry and wet ingredients – In a large bowl, mix together the dry ingredients (flour, sugar, salt), then add the foamy yeast, butter / ghee, and the milk + eggs.
  5. Mix – Start by mixing with a firm rubber spatula or a wooden spoon to bring the dough together.
  6. Bring together by hand – Once the mixture is too stiff to practically mix with a spatula, switch to hands. You don’t need to knead the dough, just mix it with your hands to bring it together into a cohesive dough.
  7. Once the dough comes together, it should be sticky and soft enough to easily come together into a ball.
  8. Proof dough – Once the butter / ghee is incorporated, shape dough into a ball. Cover with cling-wrap then put the bowl somewhere warm to let it proof – about 1 – 1.5 hours, until it has doubled in volume.
  9. Cut into six pieces – Lift the dough out of the bowl on to a lightly floured surface. Cut into 6 equal pieces. This makes ~15 – 16 cm / 6 – 6.5″ diameter naans which are a nice individual serving size and comfortably cooked in a skillet.
  10. Shape into balls – Make the top surface smooth by tucking the dough surface to the base.
  11. Rise 15 minutes, 50% increase in size – Place the balls on a lightly floured tray, and cover loosely with a a lightweight tea towel (ie just place it on top, don’t tuck it tightly under the tray). Leave to rise in a warm place for 15 minutes until they increase in size by about 50%.
  12. Flatten on lightly floured surface – Pick up a piece and flat it down lightly on a lightly floured surface.
  13. Roll out into 3 – 4mm / 0.12 – 0.16″ thick rounds (about 16cm / 6.5″ wide).
  14. Heat a well-seasoned* cast iron skillet over medium-high heat until it is just starting to smoke. Then place the naan in and leave to cook without touching.
  15. 60 to 90 seconds cooking, then flip – It should only take 60 – 90 seconds for the underside to cook until it’s nicely browned. Then use tongs to turn.
  16. Brush with ghee or butter (optionally also garlic – but yes you absolutely should!) –- Remove naan from the stove, then brush with melted ghee or butter while it’s still hot. Garlic is an optional extra, but it’s so good!
